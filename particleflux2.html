The X-ray flux (measured in W/m²) is monitored in the 0.05–0.4 nm band by NOAA's GOES satellites. This parameter is used to classify radio blackouts (NOAA R-scale), with thresholds ranging from R1 (minor) at 10⁻⁵ W/m² (M1 flare) to R5 (extreme) at 10⁻³ W/m² (X20 flare or higher).
